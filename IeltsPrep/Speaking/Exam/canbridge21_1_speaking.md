# Part 1: Haircuts

## 1. Where do you go to get a haircut?

I usually go to a local barber shop near my home because it's convenient and the barber is quite experienced. I've been going there for several years, so he already knows the hairstyle I prefer. The service is affordable, and I rarely have to wait for long.

---

## 2. Have you changed your hairstyle recently?

Not really. I've kept the same hairstyle for quite a while because it suits my face shape and is easy to maintain. Although I've thought about trying something different, I generally prefer a simple and professional look.

---

## 3. Would you ever change the colour of your hair?

Probably not. I prefer my natural hair colour because I think it looks more authentic and requires less maintenance. However, I might consider changing it in the future just for fun or to experience something new.

---

## 4. Do you enjoy going to the hairdresser/barber?

Yes, I do. I find it quite relaxing because it's an opportunity to take a break from my busy schedule. Besides getting a fresh haircut, I sometimes have interesting conversations with the barber, which makes the experience even more enjoyable.

# Part 2: Describe a Time When You Used Information for Tourists

## Band 7+ Sample Answer

I'd like to talk about a time when I used online travel information before visiting **Gangtok**, a beautiful hill station in the Indian state of Sikkim.

I got the information from several travel websites, YouTube videos, and Google Maps while planning the trip with my friends. Since it was our first visit, we wanted to understand the area and organise our itinerary in advance.

The information covered many useful aspects of the trip. It included the best tourist attractions, such as **Tsomgo Lake, Nathula Pass, MG Marg, and Rumtek Monastery**. I also found details about hotel recommendations, transportation, weather conditions, entry permits, local restaurants, estimated travel costs, and the best time to visit each attraction.

This information turned out to be extremely helpful. It helped us avoid unnecessary delays and allowed us to manage our time more efficiently. We were also able to choose a hotel in a convenient location and prepare suitable clothes because we already knew the weather would be quite cold.

Overall, I believe that using tourist information made our journey much smoother and far more enjoyable. Without it, we would probably have wasted both time and money trying to figure everything out after arriving.

# Part 3: Different Types of Holidays

## 1. What are the most popular kinds of holidays for people from your country to go on?

In Bangladesh, people generally prefer family holidays to scenic destinations such as Cox's Bazar, Sylhet, Sajek Valley, and Bandarban. Many also enjoy visiting historical sites or taking short trips during public holidays. In recent years, travelling abroad has become increasingly popular among middle-class families.

---

## 2. Do you think most people prefer to have a holiday abroad rather than in their own country?

I think it depends on their financial situation. Many people would love to travel abroad because they want to experience different cultures and lifestyles. However, domestic tourism is still more popular because it's much more affordable and convenient for most families.

---

## 3. Why do some people want to do absolutely nothing when they go away on holiday?

Many people have stressful jobs and busy lifestyles, so they simply want to relax during their holidays. Instead of following a packed schedule, they prefer sleeping late, reading books, enjoying good food, or sitting by the beach. It helps them recharge both physically and mentally.

---

# Tourist Attractions

## 4. What are the kinds of tourist attraction that visitors to your country like to see?

Bangladesh offers a wide variety of tourist attractions. Many visitors enjoy natural destinations like Cox's Bazar, the Sundarbans, Sylhet's tea gardens, and the hill tracts. Others are interested in historical landmarks, ancient mosques, archaeological sites, and cultural festivals that showcase the country's rich heritage.

---

## 5. Do you think tourist attractions such as museums should be free for local people to visit?

Personally, I think they should either be free or charge a very small entrance fee for local residents. This would encourage more people, especially students, to learn about their country's history and culture. However, charging foreign tourists a higher fee would be reasonable because it could help maintain these attractions.

---

## 6. What can make a tourist attraction disappointing for visitors?

Several factors can ruin a visitor's experience. Poor cleanliness, overcrowding, expensive entrance fees, inadequate facilities, heavy traffic, and unhelpful staff are some common problems. In addition, if a place is poorly maintained or doesn't match its online advertisements, visitors are likely to leave feeling disappointed.
