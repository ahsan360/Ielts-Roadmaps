
# IELTS Speaking Part 1 - Cities (Band 7.5+)

## Q1. How well do you know the capital city of your country?

I'd say I know **Dhaka**, the capital of Bangladesh, fairly well. Although I haven't explored every part of the city, I've visited it several times for work-related meetings and personal trips. It's a vibrant and fast-paced city with countless shopping centres, historical landmarks, and business districts. Every visit gives me a chance to discover something new.

---

## Q2. Do you think cities are exciting places to live?

Yes, absolutely. I think cities are exciting because there's always something happening. People have easy access to better educational institutions, healthcare facilities, entertainment, restaurants, and career opportunities. Moreover, city life exposes people to diverse cultures and lifestyles, which makes everyday life more interesting.

---

## Q3. Why do some people dislike living in a city?

There are several reasons. One of the biggest drawbacks is the heavy traffic congestion, which can make commuting extremely stressful. In addition, cities are often noisy, overcrowded, and polluted. Some people also find the fast-paced lifestyle exhausting and prefer the tranquillity and close-knit communities that smaller towns or villages can offer.

---

## Q4. If you could visit any city in the world, where would you go?

If I had the opportunity, I'd love to visit **Tokyo** in Japan. I've always been fascinated by how the city perfectly blends cutting-edge technology with rich cultural traditions. I'd like to experience its efficient public transportation, explore famous landmarks, try authentic Japanese cuisine, and immerse myself in its unique culture. I believe it would be an unforgettable experience.

# IELTS Speaking Part 2 (Band 7.5+)

## Describe a time when you read or heard something that you thought was not true.

### Sample Answer

I'd like to talk about a time when I came across a piece of information online that I immediately suspected was false. It happened a few months ago while I was scrolling through **Facebook** during my lunch break.

I saw a post claiming that **artificial intelligence would completely replace software engineers within the next two years**. The post quickly caught my attention because I'm a software engineer, and naturally it made me curious. It had thousands of likes and shares, and many people in the comments seemed to believe it without questioning its accuracy.

However, I found the claim highly unrealistic. From my own experience, software development involves much more than simply writing code. Developers have to communicate with clients, understand business requirements, solve unexpected problems, and make complex decisions that require creativity and critical thinking. Although AI has become an incredibly powerful tool and can automate many routine tasks, I don't believe it can entirely replace skilled software engineers in such a short period of time.

To make sure my assumption was correct, I searched for information from reliable sources and read articles written by technology experts. Most of them explained that AI is more likely to **assist developers rather than replace them completely**. That confirmed my initial opinion.

At first, I felt slightly worried because the headline sounded quite alarming. However, after doing some research, I felt relieved and realised how important it is to verify information before accepting it as fact. Since then, I've become much more cautious about believing sensational posts on social media, especially those designed to attract attention rather than provide accurate information.

Overall, this experience taught me that not everything we read online is trustworthy, and it's always wise to check reliable sources before forming an opinion.

# Honesty and Children

## Q1. Do you think children are more honest than adults?

Generally speaking, yes. I think children tend to be more honest because they usually express their thoughts and feelings without overthinking the consequences. In contrast, adults often consider social expectations, relationships, or professional responsibilities before speaking, which may lead them to hide the truth or be less direct. However, as children grow older, they gradually learn that honesty sometimes needs to be balanced with politeness and sensitivity.

---

## Q2. Why do adults tell children it's important to be honest?

Adults encourage children to be honest because honesty is one of the most important qualities for building trust and maintaining healthy relationships. When children learn to tell the truth from an early age, they are more likely to become responsible and trustworthy adults. Moreover, honesty helps them develop a strong sense of integrity and accountability.

---

## Q3. Do you think there are sometimes good reasons for adults not to tell children the truth?

Yes, in certain situations. For example, adults may choose not to reveal distressing information, such as serious family problems or a medical emergency, in order to protect a child's emotional well-being. However, I believe this should only be temporary. As children become mature enough to understand the situation, they should be told the truth in an appropriate and sensitive manner.

---

# Honesty and Advertising

## Q4. Are there any claims in advertisements that are sometimes not true?

Yes, definitely. Some advertisements exaggerate the quality or effectiveness of a product to attract customers. For instance, a skincare product might claim to produce dramatic results within a few days, even though the actual outcome varies from person to person. These exaggerated claims can create unrealistic expectations among consumers.

---

## Q5. Why do people still buy things even when they know advertisements aren't completely accurate?

There are several reasons. Firstly, advertisements are designed to appeal to people's emotions rather than their logic. Attractive visuals, celebrity endorsements, and persuasive language can strongly influence purchasing decisions. Secondly, people are often curious to try new products, hoping that the advertised benefits might actually be true. Social media trends and recommendations from influencers also play a significant role.

---

## Q6. Do you think advertisements that are dishonest should be banned?

Yes, I believe they should be strictly regulated or banned if they deliberately mislead consumers. False advertising can result in financial loss, disappointment, and even health risks, particularly when it involves medicines or health-related products. Honest marketing not only protects consumers but also encourages fair competition among businesses. Companies should be allowed to promote their products, but the information they provide should be truthful and supported by evidence.
