# IELTS Speaking Part 1: Spending and Saving Money (Band 7.5+)

## 1. When you go shopping, do you usually pay for things by cash or by card?

### Direct Answer
I usually pay by card or through mobile banking because it's much more convenient than carrying cash.

### Reason
It allows me to complete transactions quickly and keeps a digital record of my spending, which helps me manage my budget.

### Example
For example, whenever I go to a supermarket or shopping mall, I simply tap my debit card or use my mobile banking app instead of carrying cash.

### Closing
Overall, I find cashless payments more efficient and secure.

---

## 2. Are you generally careful about how much money you spend?

### Direct Answer
Yes, I'd say I'm quite careful about my spending.

### Reason
I don't like wasting money on things I don't really need, so I usually think carefully before making a purchase.

### Example
For instance, before buying an electronic device, I compare prices and read customer reviews to make sure it's worth the money.

### Closing
That's why I rarely make impulsive purchases.

---

## 3. Have you ever spent money on something you didn't need?

### Direct Answer
Yes, I have.

### Reason
Like many people, I've occasionally bought things simply because they were on sale or looked attractive.

### Example
A few months ago, I bought an expensive pair of wireless earbuds, even though my old ones were working perfectly. Later, I realised it wasn't a necessary purchase.

### Closing
Since then, I've become much more careful with my spending.

---

## 4. How important is it to you to save money for the future?

### Direct Answer
Saving money is extremely important to me.

### Reason
It provides financial security and prepares me for unexpected expenses or future goals.

### Example
For example, I try to save a portion of my monthly income so that I can travel, invest in my career, and eventually buy a house.

### Closing
I believe good saving habits lead to greater financial independence in the long run.
